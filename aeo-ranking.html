AEO — Answer Engine Optimization: How to Get Your Content Into AI Answers

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring content so it appears in direct answers — featured snippets, voice search responses, "People Also Ask" boxes, and the AI-generated summaries that now appear before organic links on over half of Google searches.

Why AEO matters: Over 60% of Google searches now trigger an AI-generated answer or featured snippet before organic links. When an answer appears, organic CTR drops 61% (Seer Interactive, Feb 2026). If your content is not optimized to BE the answer, you lose the click entirely.

The 5 AEO Schema Types That Matter Most

1. FAQPage Schema

What it does: Tells search engines "this page contains questions and answers." Sites with FAQPage schema are eligible for rich results — expandable Q&A boxes directly in search results — and are more likely to be excerpted by AI-generated answers.

How to implement: Add a JSON-LD block with FAQPage type and a mainEntity array of Question/Answer pairs. Each question should be a real question a user would ask. Each answer should be concise (2-3 sentences) and directly address the question. Do not stuff keywords — write natural Q&A pairs.

Impact: Pages with FAQPage schema appear in AI Overviews and "People Also Ask" at 3-5x the rate of pages without it. This is the single highest-impact AEO change for most sites.

2. HowTo Schema

What it does: Structures step-by-step instructional content so search engines can display it as a rich result with numbered steps, images, and estimated time. AI models extract HowTo content for procedural queries like "how to optimize for ChatGPT."

When to use: Any page that walks through a process with sequential steps. Tutorials, setup guides, recipes, and instructional articles all benefit from HowTo schema.

3. Article Schema

What it does: Declares that a page is an article — not a product page, not a landing page. This helps AI models categorize the content correctly and extract it for informational queries. Article schema should include headline, author, datePublished, and publisher information.

4. BreadcrumbList Schema

What it does: Shows the page's position in your site hierarchy directly in search results (e.g., Home > Blog > Article Title). This improves click-through rates on traditional SERPs and helps AI models understand your site structure for more accurate citation.

5. Organization + sameAs Schema

What it does: Tells AI models who you are and connects your site to verified profiles. While primarily a GEO signal, it also impacts AEO by ensuring your brand is recognized when extracted in answers. Without Organization schema, AI models may cite your content without attributing it to your brand — which means you lose the branding benefit even if your content appears.

Content Structure for Answer Engines

Beyond schema markup, how you structure your content matters enormously. AI models and answer engines extract content by reading:

  1. Heading hierarchy — H1 defines the topic, H2 defines sections, H3 defines subsections. AI models use this structure to understand what each section is about and whether it answers a specific query.
  2. Paragraph breakup — Content with 5+ short paragraphs is easier for AI to excerpt than one wall of text. Each paragraph should make one point. Aim for 2-4 sentences per paragraph.
  3. Bulleted and numbered lists — AI models extract list content more reliably than paragraphs because the structure is unambiguous. Key points, steps, and features should always be in lists.
  4. Direct answers — The first sentence after each H2 should directly answer the implied question. AI models often pull the first sentence of a section as the answer.

AEO vs GEO: Which Should You Focus On?

FactorAEOGEO
Target platformGoogle featured snippets, voice search, "People Also Ask"ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overviews
Primary signalFAQPage, HowTo, Article schemaOrganization + sameAs, OG tags, JSON-LD, content depth

Click potentialHigher — users click rich results to expand/read moreLower — users often get full answer without clicking
DifficultyLower — schema templates are straightforwardHigher — requires entity building and content investment

In practice, you need both. AEO ensures you appear in Google's answer layer. GEO ensures you appear in AI chatbots. The schemas overlap — FAQPage helps both, Organization schema helps both, and good content structure helps both. Start with AEO (faster wins), then layer on GEO (higher long-term value).
