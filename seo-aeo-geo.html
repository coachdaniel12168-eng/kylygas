SEO vs AEO vs GEO: What's the Difference and Which One Should You Focus On?

If you manage a website in 2026, you need to optimize for three different types of search engines — and they all use different signals. Understanding the distinction between SEO, AEO, and GEO is the first step to building a strategy that covers all three.

Quick summary: SEO = ranking on Google's blue links. AEO = appearing in direct answers, featured snippets, and voice search. GEO = getting cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI overviews. They overlap but each requires specific optimizations. Most sites do SEO well, AEO partially, and GEO not at all.

How They Overlap

SEO, AEO, and GEO are not separate silos. Good SEO creates the foundation that AEO and GEO build on. Here's what transfers across all three:

  • Title tags and meta descriptions — Used by Google, featured snippets, AND AI models for context. Write them once, benefit everywhere.
  • Heading structure (H1→H2→H3) — Google uses it for relevance. Answer engines use it to extract answers. AI models use it to understand content hierarchy.
  • HTTPS — Table stakes for all three. A non-HTTPS site is deprioritized everywhere.
  • Content quality — Well-written, comprehensive content ranks better on Google, gets extracted more often by answer engines, and is more likely to be cited by AI models.
  • Structured data — JSON-LD schema helps Google understand your pages, powers AEO rich results, and is the primary signal AI models use for citation decisions.

What Doesn't Transfer

Some SEO best practices have zero impact on GEO — and some GEO requirements have no SEO benefit:

  • Backlinks — Critical for SEO. Minimal impact on GEO. AI models don't crawl the link graph the way Google does.
  • sameAs links — Minimal SEO impact. Critical for GEO. AI models use these to verify entity identity.
  • Keyword density — Important for SEO. Near-irrelevant for GEO. AI models assess semantic relevance, not keyword frequency.
  • Domain age — Moderate SEO factor. Zero GEO factor. AI models don't track how long a domain has been registered.
  • Page speed — Important for SEO. Irrelevant for GEO — AI models read your content server-side and don't measure load time.

Which One Should You Prioritize?

Phase 1
SEO Foundation

Title tags, meta descriptions, H1s, HTTPS, canonical URLs, sitemap, robots.txt. Fix these first — without them nothing else works. 1-2 weeks.

Phase 2
AEO Quick Wins

Add FAQPage schema, HowTo schema, Article schema. Restructure content with clear Q&A format. 2-4 weeks.

Phase 3
GEO Investment

Organization schema with sameAs links, content depth expansion, OG tags, datePublished signals, external citation building. 4-12 weeks.

The sequence matters. You cannot do GEO effectively without clean SEO. You cannot do AEO effectively without structured content. Build the foundation, layer on the easy wins, then invest in the long-term signals that get you cited by AI.

The Competitive Advantage Right Now

As of mid-2026, approximately 65% of websites attempt SEO, about 15% have basic schema markup, and fewer than 3% actively optimize for GEO. This means the GEO landscape is dramatically less competitive than traditional SEO. A site that invests in GEO today will be cited by AI models while its competitors — even those with higher Google rankings — remain invisible to the fastest-growing search channel.

This window will close as more businesses discover GEO. The sites that build entity signals, structured data, and citation patterns now will be the default sources AI models cite for years to come — because AI models develop persistent citation habits.
