GEO Optimization Guide: How to Get Cited by ChatGPT in 2026

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your website so AI models like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews cite your content. In 2026, 1 billion people use ChatGPT monthly. 48-55% of Google searches show AI answers before links. If your content isn't AI-optimized, you're invisible to a growing share of your market.

The 5 GEO Signals That Matter Most

  1. Structured Data (JSON-LD): AI models parse structured data for direct answers. FAQPage, HowTo, Article, and Organization schema are the most impactful. Without them, your content is just raw text to AI.
  2. Entity Signals (sameAs): Links to LinkedIn, Wikipedia, Crunchbase, and social profiles tell AI models you're a real organization. Anonymous sites get skipped by AI-generated answers.
  3. Content Depth: AI prefers pages with 500+ words, clear heading hierarchy (H1→H2→H3), and bulleted lists. Thin content gets ignored regardless of writing quality.
  4. Citation Patterns: Sites that link to authoritative sources and display clear author bylines get cited more. Attribution signals trust to AI models.
  5. Freshness Signals: dateModified and regular content updates tell AI your information is current. Stale content drops in citation frequency.

Quick GEO Checklist

  • Do you have JSON-LD structured data? (Test with Google's Rich Results Test)
  • Do you have Organization schema with sameAs links?
  • Is your content 500+ words with clear H2 headings?
  • Do your pages have FAQPage or HowTo schema where relevant?
  • Are Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description) present?
  • Do blog posts show author name and bio?
  • Is your site using HTTPS?
