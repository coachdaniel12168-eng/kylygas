What government grants are available for SMEs in Singapore?

Singapore's government — through agencies like Enterprise Singapore (ESG), IMDA, Ministry of Manpower (MOM), and SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG) — administers a comprehensive ecosystem of funding programmes. These are not loans you must repay — most are outright grants, co-funding arrangements, wage offsets, and tax credits designed to strengthen your business.

The grants fall into 8 major categories:

  1. Capability Development — EDG (S$1M), MRA (S$100K)
  2. Digital Transformation — PSG (S$30K), SMEs Go Digital, Digital Leaders Programme (S$100K), GenAI Navigator
  3. Financing & Loans — Enterprise Financing Scheme (up to S$50M government-backed loans)
  4. Sustainability — Sustainability Reporting Grant (S$35K), Enterprise Sustainability Programme (S$50K)
  5. Workforce & Hiring — Senior Employment Credit (up to 8% wage offset), Career Conversion Programme (S$36K salary support), Work-Life Grant (S$105K)
  6. Training & Upskilling — SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit (S$10K), NTUC CTC Grant (S$350K), Training Grant Scheme (up to 90% subsidy)
  7. AI & Innovation — Open Innovation Platform (S$200K), GenAI Navigator (S$30K)
  8. Internationalisation — MRA (S$100K), EDG Market Access pillar

How much EDG funding can I get for my business?

The Enterprise Development Grant (EDG) provides up to 70% co-funding on qualifying costs, with maximum support of S$1 million per project. This is Singapore's flagship grant for business transformation.

What the EDG covers:

  • Core Capabilities — Business strategy, financial management, HR development, service excellence
  • Innovation & Productivity — Product development, process improvement, automation, standards adoption
  • Market Access — Overseas market entry, M&A advisory, pilot projects in new markets

EDG eligibility requirements:

  • Registered and operating in Singapore
  • At least 30% local shareholding (Singaporean or PR)
  • Financially viable and able to co-fund at least 30% of the project
  • Clear project scope, deliverables, and timeline

Typical EDG grants range from S$30,000 to S$1 million per project. Most SME applications fall in the S$50K–S$300K range. The application requires a detailed project proposal with milestones, which is where GrantBot's done-for-you EDG application service makes the difference between approval and rejection.

Who qualifies for the PSG grant in Singapore?

The Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) is the most popular SME grant in Singapore — and for good reason. It is faster, simpler, and covers off-the-shelf digital solutions and equipment that businesses buy anyway.

PSG eligibility checklist:

  • ✅ Registered and operating in Singapore
  • ✅ At least 30% local shareholding
  • ✅ SME status — annual turnover ≤ S$100 million OR ≤ 200 employees
  • ✅ Purchasing a pre-approved solution from the PSG list

PSG funding rates:

  • Standard solutions: 50% co-funding (CRM, ERP, HRMS, accounting, POS, cybersecurity)
  • Advanced solutions: 70% co-funding (AI, robotics, advanced analytics)
  • Maximum: S$30,000 per solution per year

The IMDA SMEs Go Digital programme maintains the pre-approved list with over 250 solutions across 20+ industry-specific digital plans. GrantBot maintains a curated list of PSG-eligible solutions for 2026 updated monthly.

What other Singapore SME grants should I know about in 2026?

Beyond the flagship EDG and PSG, Singapore offers several high-value schemes that fewer businesses know about — meaning less competition and faster approvals:

Market Readiness Assistance (MRA) — up to S$100K

If you are expanding overseas, the MRA grant covers up to 70% of market setup costs: overseas market assessment, legal and documentation fees, business matching, and in-market promotion. Maximum S$100,000 per company per new market, with up to 2 applications per market.

Enterprise Financing Scheme (EFS) — up to S$50M

The EFS is a government-backed loan scheme where Enterprise Singapore shares 50–70% of the loan risk with participating banks. This means lower interest rates and easier approval. Seven loan types: Trade (up to S$10M), Working Capital (S$500K), Fixed Assets, Project, Venture Debt, M&A (up to S$50M), and Green Loan.

Sustainability Reporting Grant — up to S$35K

With ESG requirements expanding, the Sustainability Reporting Grant co-funds your first sustainability report at 70%, capped at S$35,000. Covers consultancy for ESG disclosure aligned with international standards like GRI, TCFD, and ISSB.

GenAI Navigator (NEW for 2026) — up to S$30K

The GenAI Navigator is a brand-new 2026 programme by IMDA and Enterprise Singapore to accelerate SME adoption of Generative AI. Includes a free AI readiness assessment, curated GenAI solution recommendations, and PSG co-funding of up to 50% for qualifying AI solutions.

Career Conversion Programme (CCP) — up to S$36K salary support

Hiring mid-career workers? The WSG Career Conversion Programme provides up to 90% salary support (capped at S$6,000/month for 6 months = S$36,000) plus course fee subsidies of 70–90%. You reskill the worker while the government covers most of the cost.

Senior Employment Credit (SEC) — up to 8% wage offset

The Senior Employment Credit is automatic — no application needed. If you employ Singaporean workers aged 55+ earning ≤ S$4,000/month, you receive wage offsets: 2% (ages 55–59), 3% (60–64), 5% (65–66), and 8% (67+). Disbursed directly by CPF Board.

How do I apply for Singapore government grants?

The application process varies by grant, but the general workflow is:

  1. Check eligibility — Verify local shareholding (≥30%), SME status, and specific grant requirements. GrantBot's free 60-second scan does this automatically across all 34 grants.
  2. Prepare documents — ACRA business profile, financial statements, project proposal (for EDG), quotations (for PSG), and supporting evidence like invoices and CVs of project team.
  3. Submit application — Via the Enterprise Singapore Business Grants Portal or relevant agency portal.
  4. Claims disbursement — Most grants operate on reimbursement: you pay first, submit claims with receipts, and receive co-funding. Typical processing time: 6–12 weeks.
