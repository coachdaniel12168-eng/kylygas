## COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE

### TIER 1: Human Consultancies (Most Popular)

---

#### 1. Accounting Firms (Big 4 + Mid-Tier)
**Type:** Human | **Scale:** High

| Firm | Grant Service | Est. Fees |
|---|---|---|
| PwC Singapore | EDG/PSG consulting, innovation grants | S$8K-25K per application |
| KPMG Singapore | Government grant advisory, EDG, tax incentives | S$10K-30K |
| EY Singapore | EDG, PSG, EIS tax claims | S$8K-25K |
| Deloitte Singapore | Grant consulting, R&D tax, EDG | S$8K-25K |
| RSM Singapore | SME grant applications, PSG specialist | S$5K-15K |
| BDO Singapore | SME grants, EDG, PSG | S$5K-12K |

**Strengths:**
- Established trust — "if PwC says you qualify, you qualify"
- Deep understanding of audit trails and compliance
- Can bundle with accounting/tax services
- Existing client base of SMEs

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive — minimum engagement S$5K
- Slow — big firm processes, 2-4 week turnaround
- Won't touch small applications (sub-S$50K grants not worth their time)
- Not tech-forward — manual processes, email/PDF based
- Often reject clients who "aren't big enough"

---

#### 2. Boutique Grant Consultants
**Type:** Human | **Scale:** Medium

Examples: GrantAssist, various independent consultants on LinkedIn

| Attributes | Details |
|---|---|
| Typical fee | S$3K-8K flat OR 10-20% of grant amount |
| Specialisation | Usually 1-2 grant types (EDG only, or PSG only) |
| Marketing | Word of mouth, LinkedIn, SME events |
| Turnaround | 1-2 weeks |

**Strengths:**
- More affordable than Big 4
- Personal relationship, single point of contact
- Faster turnaround
- Will take smaller clients

**Weaknesses:**
- Inconsistent quality — anyone can call themselves a grant consultant
- Often one-person operations — capacity limited
- May not stay updated on policy changes
- Limited to 1-2 grant types, not comprehensive
- No tech platform — everything via email/WhatsApp

---

#### 3. PSG Pre-Approved Vendors (IMDA List)
**Type:** Human/Tech hybrid | **Scale:** Large ecosystem

IMDA pre-approves vendors who can offer PSG-subsidised solutions. Many of these vendors ALSO offer grant application assistance as a value-add.

**Strengths:**
- Already in the government ecosystem
- Can bundle "buy our software + we help apply for the PSG grant to pay for it"
- Trusted by IMDA

**Weaknesses:**
- Grant help is a side service, not their core
- Only cover PSG, not EDG or other grants
- Conflict of interest — they recommend their own solution
- Quality of grant writing varies enormously

### TIER 2: AI/Tech Platforms

---

#### 4. Grantify (UK-based, attempted SG)
**Type:** AI-assisted | **Status:** Pulled out of SG (404 on SG page)

**Original Model:**
- AI matching algorithm for grant eligibility
- Guided application writing with templates
- UK/EU focus, briefly expanded to Singapore

**Weaknesses:**
- Not built for Singapore's specific grant landscape
- Generic AI that didn't understand local nuances (CPF, local shareholding rules)
- Withdrew from SG market
- **Key lesson: Generic AI platforms fail in Singapore without localisation**

---

#### 5. Government Portals (BGP / GoBusiness)
**Type:** Self-service | **Scale:** Universal | **Cost:** Free

The official Business Grants Portal and GoBusiness platform.

**Strengths:**
- Official, trusted, free
- Direct submission to government
- All grants listed

**Weaknesses:**
- You're on your own — no guidance, no handholding
- Complex forms, technical language
- No eligibility pre-checking — you fill everything in THEN find out you don't qualify
- Zero marketing — they don't reach out to SMEs
- **This is your biggest advantage — government portals are comprehensive but user-hostile**

---

#### 6. AI Grant Matching Tools (Emerging)
**Type:** AI | **Scale:** Small/Nascent

Several small startups and side projects offer AI-powered grant matching for Singapore:
- GrantMatch (basic keyword matching)
- Various Telegram bots scraping government sites
- A few no-code directories listing grants

**Strengths:**
- Fast, free discovery
- Low barrier to try

**Weaknesses:**
- Shallow — keyword matching, not eligibility analysis
- No application writing — discovery only
- Not updated (many still list PIC and other dead grants)
- No country-specific logic
- No monetisation path — these are hobby projects
