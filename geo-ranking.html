How to Rank in GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing web content so that generative AI models — like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's Gemini — can find, understand, and cite your information when generating answers.

Unlike traditional SEO which targets search engine ranking algorithms, GEO targets the language models that power AI-generated responses. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best tool for AI search visibility," your goal is to be the source it cites.

How GEO Differs From SEO

  • SEO optimizes for crawlers that index and rank pages based on backlinks, keywords, and authority
  • GEO optimizes for language models that extract and synthesize information from multiple sources
  • GEO requires citation-ready content — original data, clear attribution, and quotable passages

5 GEO Ranking Factors

  1. Original Research & Data: AI models prioritize unique statistics, studies, and proprietary data
  2. Entity Consistency: Your brand, authors, and products must be consistently referenced across the web
  3. Passage-Level Clarity: Each section should stand alone as a coherent, extractable unit
  4. Authoritative Citations: Being cited by other authoritative sources signals trustworthiness to AI
  5. AI Crawler Accessibility: Ensure your robots.txt allows AI-specific crawlers like GPTBot and Claude-Web
