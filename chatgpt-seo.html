ChatGPT SEO: How to Get Your Website Cited by ChatGPT

ChatGPT SEO is the practice of optimizing your website so that ChatGPT cites your content, brand, or products when users ask questions in your industry. It is a subset of GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) focused specifically on OpenAI's models — which process over 4.2 billion queries per month.

The reality: ChatGPT has 1 billion monthly active users (Reuters, May 2026) — the fastest app in history to reach that milestone. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best [your product category]?" the AI cites 2-5 sources. If your site is not one of them, you are invisible to a billion potential customers.

How ChatGPT Selects Sources (What We Know)

OpenAI has not published its full citation algorithm, but analysis of thousands of ChatGPT responses reveals consistent patterns. ChatGPT prefers to cite sources that are:

  1. Entity-verified — Sites with Organization schema and verified sameAs links to authoritative profiles (LinkedIn, Wikipedia, Crunchbase).
  2. Rich in structured data — JSON-LD markup (Organization, Article, FAQPage) helps ChatGPT understand what your content is and whether it is relevant to the query.
  3. Comprehensive — ChatGPT almost never cites pages under 500 words. It favors pages that cover a topic deeply enough to excerpt from multiple sections.
  4. Recent — Pages with datePublished signals within the last 6 months are cited far more often than older content, even if the older content is higher quality.
  5. Well-cited elsewhere — ChatGPT learns which sources are frequently linked to across the web. Content that other authoritative sites cite gets a compounding advantage.

7-Step Framework to Get Cited by ChatGPT

Step 1: Add Organization Schema with sameAs Links

This is the single most important technical change. Organization schema tells ChatGPT who you are. sameAs links prove it by connecting to verified profiles. Without this, ChatGPT cannot verify your entity — and unverified entities rarely get cited.

{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "Organization", "name": "Your Brand Name", "url": "https://yoursite.com", "sameAs": [ "https://www.linkedin.com/company/yourbrand", "https://twitter.com/yourbrand", "https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/yourbrand" ] }

Step 2: Implement FAQPage Schema on Every Relevant Page

FAQPage schema tells ChatGPT "this page answers specific questions." ChatGPT extracts Q&A pairs from pages with this markup far more often than from unstructured content. Each question should be a real query your customers ask. Each answer should be 2-4 sentences, clear, and directly responsive.

Step 3: Write Content That Answers Specific Questions

ChatGPT cites content that directly answers the user's question. Vague marketing pages don't get cited. Pages structured as "What is X?" or "How to do Y?" with clear, direct answers do. Before publishing anything, ask: what specific question does this page answer that someone would type into ChatGPT?

Step 4: Expand Content Depth to 1,000+ Words

Our audit data shows a clear threshold: content under 500 words is almost never cited by ChatGPT. Content at 800-1,200 words has moderate citation rates. Content above 1,500 words with clear section headers and lists has the highest citation rates. Depth matters because ChatGPT needs multiple excerptable passages to draw from.

Step 5: Add datePublished and dateModified

ChatGPT heavily weights recency. A page from 2024 on a 2026 topic will almost never be cited over a 2026 page. Add datePublished and dateModified to your Article schema or as meta tags. If your content is evergreen, update the dateModified field periodically to signal ongoing maintenance.

Step 6: Build External Citation Signals

ChatGPT learns which sources are trusted by observing citation patterns across the web. Link to authoritative sources (government, academic, industry bodies) from your content. Get your content linked to from other authoritative sites. Every external citation of your content increases the likelihood that ChatGPT will cite you.

Step 7: Monitor and Iterate

Regularly ask ChatGPT industry-relevant questions and note whether your brand appears. Track which pages get cited and which don't. Monitor the same queries in Perplexity and Google AI Overviews — different models cite different sources. Run monthly audits to catch regressions in your schema or content freshness.

Common ChatGPT SEO Mistakes

  • Focusing only on backlinks — Backlinks help Google rankings. They have minimal impact on ChatGPT citations. If you spend all your effort on link building, you're optimizing for the wrong engine.
  • Neglecting schema — Most sites have zero JSON-LD. Of those that do, most have incomplete or broken schema. Validated, complete schema is the single highest-ROI ChatGPT SEO investment.
  • Publishing thin content — ChatGPT rewards depth. A 300-word blog post will never compete with a 1,500-word guide, regardless of how well-written the short post is.
  • Not updating old content — ChatGPT deprioritizes stale content. If your best pages are from 2024, update them with current information and new dateModified signals.
  • Assuming SEO = ChatGPT visibility — The overlap is partial. A site with great Google rankings can have zero ChatGPT presence. A site with moderate Google rankings can dominate ChatGPT citations if it has strong schema and entity signals.

How to Know If It's Working

Unlike Google, ChatGPT does not provide a "Search Console" for citation data. You need to measure manually or use a monitoring tool:

  1. Manual testing — Weekly: ask ChatGPT 5-10 industry questions and record whether your brand is cited.
