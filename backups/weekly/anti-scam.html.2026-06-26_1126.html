🛡️ Anti-Scam & Anti-Fraud Guide

How to protect yourself from grant scams and fake consultants.

How to Spot a Grant Scam

🚩 RED FLAG: "Guaranteed approval"

No legitimate consultant can guarantee government grant approval. All decisions are made by government agencies. Anyone who guarantees approval is lying.

🚩 RED FLAG: Asking for your SingPass/CorpPass password

Government officials and legitimate consultants will NEVER ask for your login passwords. They may ask you to log in yourself, but never to share your credentials.

🚩 RED FLAG: "Pay now to reserve your grant"

Government grants are not reserved with upfront payments. You apply through official portals. Anyone asking for a "reservation fee" or "processing fee" payable to a personal account is a scammer.

🚩 RED FLAG: "We're from the government"

Government officers identify themselves with official credentials. They do not cold-call businesses offering grants. If someone claims to be "from the government grants department" and you didn't contact them first — be suspicious.

🚩 RED FLAG: Pressure to act immediately

"This grant closes tomorrow — pay now!" Legitimate consultants give you time to review. Scammers create false urgency.

🚩 RED FLAG: No company registration, no physical address

Check if the consultancy is a registered company. GovFund is operated by ScaleSMEs, a registered Singapore company. Scam operations often have no verifiable business registration.

Verify Before You Pay

Before engaging any grant consultant:

  1. Check their company registration number
  2. Look for a physical business address
  3. Read independent reviews (not just their own website)
  4. Verify they clearly state they are NOT a government agency
  5. Check if their pricing is published transparently
  6. Never pay to a personal bank account — only to a registered company account
