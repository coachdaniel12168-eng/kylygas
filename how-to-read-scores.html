Understanding Your AI Search Visibility Scores

This page explains in plain English what each score means, why it matters, and how to improve it — the foundation of any AI search audit. No jargon — just what you need to know.

Quick summary: AI search visibility measures how likely ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews are to cite your website when someone asks a question in your industry. The higher your score, the more AI-ready your site is.

Overall Score (X/100)

Your overall score is the average of 7 dimensions. Think of it like a school report card — each dimension is a subject, and your overall score is your GPA.

Score RangeWhat It Means
70-100Strong AI presence. Your site sends most of the signals AI models look for. You're likely already appearing in AI-generated answers for your industry.
45-69Room for improvement. Your site has some AI-friendly elements but is missing key signals. Competitors with higher scores are more likely to be cited.
0-44At risk. Your site is mostly invisible to AI search engines. AI models cannot reliably extract or cite your content. Significant gaps need addressing.

The 7 Dimensions Explained

1. SEO — Traditional Search Optimization

What it checks: Title tags, meta descriptions, H1 headings, canonical URLs, HTTPS, image alt text.

Why it matters: These are the fundamentals. If Google can't understand your page, neither can an AI model. Think of SEO as making sure your content has a clear label and description. Without these basics, everything else is harder.

Quick fix: Add a title tag (50-65 characters) and a meta description (120-155 characters) to every page. These are the first things both Google and AI models read.

2. AEO — Answer Engine Optimization

What it checks: FAQPage schema, HowTo schema, Article structure, BreadcrumbList, paragraph breakup.

Why it matters: Answer engines (Google's featured snippets, voice search, Siri, Alexa) need content structured as clear questions and answers. Without FAQ or HowTo markup, your content gets skipped when someone asks "what is..." or "how do I..."

Quick fix: Add FAQPage structured data to any page with Q&A content. This is the single highest-impact change for appearing in Google's "People also ask" and AI Overviews.

3. GEO — Generative Engine Optimization

What it checks: Open Graph tags, JSON-LD structured data, Organization schema, word count, freshness signals.

Why it matters: ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude read your page differently than Google. They look for structured data (JSON-LD), authorship signals, and comprehensive content (500+ words). Without these, your site might as well be invisible to AI chatbots.

Quick fix: Add Open Graph tags and JSON-LD Organization schema. These tell AI models who you are and what your page is about — the AI equivalent of introducing yourself at a networking event.

4. Entity — Brand Identity Signals

What it checks: sameAs links (social profiles, Wikipedia), Organization schema with entity declarations, location/address signals.

Why it matters: AI models build a "knowledge graph" of who you are. If your site has sameAs links connecting it to your LinkedIn, Wikipedia, or Crunchbase profile, the AI treats you as a verified, trustworthy entity. Without them, you're anonymous text — and AI models are reluctant to cite anonymous sources.

Quick fix: Add sameAs links in your Organization schema pointing to your LinkedIn company page, Twitter, and any Wikipedia or Crunchbase entry. This is the #1 entity signal AI models use.

5. Structure — Content Organization

What it checks: H2 subheadings, bulleted/numbered lists, mobile viewport, language declaration.

Why it matters: AI models extract content by reading your heading hierarchy and pulling from structured elements like lists. Walls of unbroken paragraphs are hard for AI to excerpt cleanly. Well-structured content with H2s and bullet points gets cited more often because the AI can pull a clean, coherent excerpt.

Quick fix: Add H2 subheadings every 200-300 words and use bulleted lists for key points. Think of it as making your content "scoopable" for AI.

6. Citation — Trustworthiness Signals

What it checks: Author metadata, external links to authoritative sources, blockquotes, title descriptiveness.

Why it matters: AI models prefer to cite content with clear authorship and good citation hygiene. If you link to authoritative sources (government sites, research, industry bodies), the AI sees you as part of a credible information ecosystem. Anonymous content with no external links looks like spam to an AI.

Quick fix: Add author bylines and link to 3+ authoritative external sources per page. Show the AI you're part of a credible conversation, not shouting into the void.

7. Crawler — Accessibility to AI

What it checks: Robots meta tag, noindex status, sitemap references, text-to-code ratio.

Why it matters: If your page has a noindex tag or a very low text-to-code ratio (too much JavaScript, not enough readable text), AI crawlers can't access or understand your content. This is the plumbing — boring but essential.

Quick fix: Check for noindex tags (remove them if unintentional), add a sitemap.xml, and reduce inline CSS/JS by moving to external files.
